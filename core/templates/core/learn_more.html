How CardFolio Works

CardFolio helps you track your Magic: The Gathering cards as real investments. Below you’ll find a quick guide to understand how to use the app and how each action affects your portfolio.

1. Adding a card to your collection

To add a card, you need two identifiers printed on every Magic card:

  • Set code – identifies the expansion (e.g. ECL)
  • Collector number – identifies the exact printing (e.g. 320)

These two values guarantee that CardFolio imports the exact printing from Scryfall — including artwork, rarity. Check the Foil version checkbox if you card has a foil finish.

Purchase price. It represents how much you paid per card and is used later to calculate:

  • Total Invested
  • ROI
  • Profit & Loss

2. Selling a card

Selling a card creates a SELL transaction. You choose:

  • How many copies to sell
  • The price per unit you sold them for

When a card is sold:

  • Your collection quantity is reduced
  • A transaction is permanently stored
  • Realized profit/loss is updated

If you sell your last copy, the card disappears from your collection — but the transaction history remains.

3. Understanding the dashboard

The dashboard summarizes your collection as a portfolio. Each metric answers a different question:

  • Total Invested – money spent buying cards
  • Total Earned – money received from sold cards
  • Current Portfolio Value – market value of cards you still own
  • Unrealized P/L – paper gains or losses
  • ROI – performance of cards you still hold
  • Total ROI – overall performance including sold cards

Market prices are updated daily using Scryfall bulk data.
